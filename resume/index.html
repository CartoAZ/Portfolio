UW Pepperell Lab Cartographer & GIS Analyst

2015 - 2016 | Madison, WI

The Pepperell Lab studies the ecology and evolution of infectious diseases. I am working on a phylogeography project that identified several lineages of Mycobacterium tuberculosis in modern samples and traced each back to a common ancestor ca. 700 BCE. Ancient trade, in particular the Silk Road, was thought to have been a means of transportation for the disease, so I performed network analysis using modern M. tuberculosis isolate locations and ancient trade routes to help determine the spatial patterns of genetic variation among the isolates. I am also creating several static maps for publication, which show the location of isolates in respect to ancient trade routes, as well as an interactive map that shows the spread of the disease through time.
